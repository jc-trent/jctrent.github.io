About the Author

J.C. Trent writes progression fantasy and science fiction, mostly because he can't stop asking questions like "if your punch can shatter a mountain, why didn't you go flying?" and "would a cultivator suffocate in a vacuum?" The answers to these questions are never simple, and by the time he's figured them out, he's accidentally written a magic system with a spreadsheet and a novel with too many load-bearing side characters.

When he's not stress-testing fictional physics, he's building overcomplicated build pipelines for his novels, because refactoring a few thousand lines of Python is way more productive than actually reviewing the line edits he paid a truly unreasonable amount of money for.

He is a software engineer by trade, a writer by compulsion, and a resident of Phoenix, Arizona, where the weather is trying to kill him in a completely different way than his books.
